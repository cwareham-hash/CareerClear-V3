1. Executive Summary

Career Clear is an education technology platform designed to help college-age students, high school students, and young professionals discover and deeply understand career opportunities. The platform directly addresses two critical, validated problems: users' limited awareness of the full range of available roles, and their lack of experiential understanding of day-to-day realities within specific roles.

The platform features two levels of engagement:

  • Exploration: Browse career overviews — role descriptions, required skills, typical education backgrounds, AI impact outlook, and sample workday summaries.
  • Exploitation (Simulation): Immersive 5-day work week simulations where users virtually shadow a professional through authentic, time-blocked experiences across four selectable duration tiers.

The MVP targets web and mobile interfaces with simplified name-based authentication and four fully-developed career simulations: Investment Banking Analyst, Management Consultant, Law Associate, and Product Manager.

2. Problem Statement

2.1 Limited Career Awareness

Users rely on fragmented sources — personal networks, Google searches, scattered online platforms — that fail to provide comprehensive visibility into available career paths. Students frequently report not knowing what roles exist, or how roles within the same industry differ meaningfully from one another. This leads to significant opportunity cost: students miss roles that would be strong fits simply due to lack of exposure.

2.2 Superficial Understanding

Even when users identify potential career paths, they lack detailed insight into day-to-day realities. Generic job descriptions fail to capture what it actually feels like to perform a role — the cadence, the pressures, the interactions, and the decision-making moments. This gap leads to poor career choices and post-hire dissatisfaction. Career Clear's simulation layer directly addresses this by providing authentic, immersive work experiences before any commitment is made.

3. Solution Overview

3.1 Exploration Level

A consolidated, searchable platform displaying careers organized by industry and function. Each career card presents: brief role description, required skills, typical educational backgrounds, high-level sample workday, AI impact and growth outlook, and sample companies.

3.2 Exploitation Level (Simulation)

Immersive 5-day work week simulations with varying detail based on selected duration tier:

DurationLabelDescription
10 minutesKey highlightsMost impactful moments only — 4–6 time blocks
30 minutesMain activitiesCore activities across the week — 8–12 time blocks
2 hoursDetailed view2–3 representative days in moderate detail
5 hoursFull immersionComplete 5-day work week — all time blocks

4. Target Users

4.1 Primary Audience

  • College students (undergraduate and graduate) actively exploring career paths
  • High school students beginning early career research
  • Young professionals (0–3 years experience) considering career pivots

4.2 User Needs

  • Comprehensive, consolidated overview of available career options
  • Realistic preview of daily work experiences beyond generic job descriptions
  • Self-assessment tools to evaluate personal role fit
  • Confidence in career decisions before investing significant time or resources
  • A structured way to compare multiple careers over time

5. Core Features & Functionality

5.1 User Authentication

5.1.1 MVP Approach

Simple name-based account creation — no email or password required for V1. On first visit, a centered modal appears prompting the user to enter their name. The modal has a single text input, a teal "Get Started" CTA, and a brief value proposition line ("Track your career exploration journey"). Returning users are recognized via localStorage and taken directly to the landing page.

  • User enters their name to create or access their account
  • Account persists progress, favorites, and simulation completions
  • No password reset, no email verification — intentionally frictionless for MVP
  • Future: migrate to Auth0 or Firebase Auth with email/social login

5.2 Landing Page

5.2.1 Hero Section

Full-width hero with a deep navy-to-teal gradient background (#1a2744 → #2a9d8f). Contains: large serif headline ("Discover Your Future Career"), 2-line subtitle in muted teal, and a primary CTA button ("Take Career Quiz") in teal with white text and a sparkle icon. Hero height: 340px desktop / 280px mobile.

5.2.2 Career Grid

  • Full-width search bar with placeholder "Search careers..." and magnifier icon
  • Horizontal filter pill row: Industry (Finance, Consulting, Legal, Technology, Marketing, Sales) and Function (Analytical, Client-Facing, Creative, Technical)
  • Results count label ("10 careers found")
  • 3-column grid on desktop, 2-column on tablet, 1-column on mobile
  • Each card: white bg, teal top border (4px), icon top-left, heart favorite top-right, title, 2-sentence description, skill tags, industry + Simulation badges, "Explore →" link

5.3 Onboarding Questionnaire

Typeform-style one-question-at-a-time flow with animated transitions. Maximum 20 questions across: work style preferences, location preferences, industry interests, education level, and skills/strengths. Results displayed as personalized career cards and saved to Dashboard for comparison across retakes.

5.4 Career Card — Summary Level

Full detail page with: 2–3 paragraph description, required skills list, educational backgrounds (majors + graduate degrees), sample workday overview, AI impact & growth outlook, sample companies (secondary, non-prominent), and prominent "Explore Detailed Simulation →" CTA at bottom.

5.5 Simulation Interface

5.5.1 Overview Section

Simulation title (serif headline), scenario context paragraph, and duration selector component above the calendar.

5.5.2 Duration Selector

Four equal-width pill buttons. Each pill shows duration (bold) on line 1 and label (muted) on line 2. Selected state: filled navy/teal bg with white text. Selection dynamically updates calendar view.

5.5.3 Weekly Calendar View

  • 5-column grid (Mon–Fri), each column: day name header + "Day N" subtitle
  • Time block cards: colored left-border accent, play icon, time range (muted), activity title (bold)
  • Active block: teal border highlight, "You are here" pill badge
  • Completed blocks: checkmark icon, reduced opacity
  • Hover: shadow elevation + 2px translateY
  • Progress bar above calendar: "X of Y activities completed" + % right-aligned

5.5.4 Time Block Experience

Click opens a right-side panel (desktop, 480px wide) or full-screen modal (mobile). Three sections:

  • Before: context explaining what's about to happen
  • During: Simulated Work (standard text) + Over-the-Shoulder Commentary (teal-bordered box, italic, "Commentary" badge)
  • After: summary + transition to next activity
  • Footer: "Mark Complete" CTA + "Next Activity →" link

5.6 Feedback & Rating System

Post-simulation 1–10 rating modal with anchor labels (1 = "not for me" / 10 = "perfect fit"). Optional text feedback via templates: What I liked, What I didn't like, Questions I still have, How this compares. Each duration tier tracked separately; dashboard shows rating evolution.

5.7 User Dashboard

  • Main view: completed simulations with ratings, completed duration indicators, favorites
  • Detailed role view: all ratings, text feedback, timestamps, re-take link
  • Questionnaire tab: all attempts, recommended roles, retake option
  • Favorites: "Saved to Try" (not yet simulated) vs. "Actively Pursuing" (post-positive simulation)

5.8 Returning User Experience

  • Land on career exploration page (not dashboard)
  • Career cards show visual "explored" indicators (completed badge or muted overlay)
  • Resume simulation prompt if in-progress simulation exists

7. User Interface Requirements

7.1 Platform Requirements

  • Web-responsive (desktop and tablet)
  • Mobile-responsive (smartphone)
  • Modern, clean, professional design aesthetic
  • WCAG AA accessibility compliance

  • Clear visual hierarchy — serif display headlines, clean sans-serif body
  • Consistent color and type system applied uniformly
  • Confidence-inspiring — design should feel professional and trustworthy to a college student

  • Center: Explore Careers | Questionnaire | My Dashboard
  • Right: "Hi, [Name]" + Sign Out

  • Mobile: hamburger → full-width drawer

12.2 Sample Time Block — Investment Banking

Monday, 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM: Client Pitch Preparation

Before Activity Context

You're working on a pitch book for a potential M&A advisory engagement. The client is a mid-sized retail company considering acquiring a smaller competitor. The Vice President asked you to refine the financial analysis and update valuation models based on the latest comparable transactions. The pitch meeting is tomorrow afternoon.

During Activity — Simulated Work

[Scene: Analyst working at desk with dual monitors showing Excel and PowerPoint]

Analyst (internal monologue): "Need to update the comparable company analysis with last night's earnings releases. Two of our comps reported, which could shift the valuation multiples."

[Phone rings — VP calling]

VP: "Hey, quick question on the pitch deck. Can you walk me through the synergy assumptions on slide 15? The MD wants to make sure we're being conservative."

Analyst: "Sure. I assumed 15% cost synergies based on the overlap in their distribution networks and back-office functions. I cross-referenced this with the three most recent retail M&A transactions, which averaged between 12–18%."

VP: "Good. Can you also add a sensitivity table showing how the valuation changes if synergies come in at 10% or 20%? Client will want to see the range."

Analyst: "Absolutely. I'll have that to you by end of day."

Commentary

What just happened: The VP is quality-checking the analysis before it goes to the MD and ultimately to the client. This is typical — every assumption needs to be defensible and benchmarked against real market data. The sensitivity analysis request is standard practice to show clients multiple scenarios.

Key skills in action: You need to be able to quickly explain your work, defend your assumptions with data, and adapt to feedback in real-time. Building credibility with senior bankers requires being thorough and responsive.

After Activity Context

You spend the rest of the morning updating the Excel model with the sensitivity analysis and refreshing the PowerPoint slides. The updated deck needs review by the VP by 6 PM. Next: a team lunch meeting to discuss the broader pitch strategy and prepare for potential client questions.
